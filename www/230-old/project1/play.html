Mastering the Guitar

Practice (and performance) makes perfect!

If you've got the guitarist's toolset underneath your belt, you just need to practice and perform in order to ensure you've mastered the guitar.

How can we improve our skills!

1. Follow the beat
(and master your rhythm).

Rhythm refers to a strong, regular, repeated pattern of movement or sound; in music this refers to the systematic arrangement of sounds. As you develop an ear for music, finding the rhythm inside of a song's melody will pay great dividends toward your mastery of the guitar.

2. Memorize songs
(and play till your heart sings).

By memorizing the melodies for your favorite songs, you'll get better at playing without sheet music and tablature in front of you. The rhythm of a song can often act as a marker in a song to help you keep your place.

Keep up your skills by learning your favorite songs or finding sheet music for simple songs. Some suggestions include: "Redemption Song," by Bob Marley, "Love Me Do," by The Beatles, and "Stay With Me," by Sam Smith

3. Record yourself
(and watch it all over again).

Any performer knows that by recording themselves, they can note any issues with their act.

Record yourself when you play. It will help you in identifying any physical issues that the guitar may be presenting and it will give you the chance to review your own work. If you do well, you could even upload a video to YouTube!

4. Perform at Open Mics
(and do it solo).

Open Mics are a great way for you to get your name out as an artist as well as get comfortable with a live audience in a low-pressure environment.

Practice doesn't just stop in your sound-proofed room, studio, or garage. Publicly performing is an important part to taking your skills to the next level.

6. Play with others
(and maybe start a band)?

Playing alone is a great way to focus on your individual skill, but, by playing with others you can learn to accomodate for others. Once you can play alone, try jamming out with other musicians you know.

When playing with others, you can get used to working together to create better music and write better pieces. Having others to draw inspiration from, collaborate with, and jam with will make you a better artist, every time.

 

That's it!

Practice does indeed make perfect!

It takes 10,000 hours to master any skill! Stick to it and you could be the next Jimi Hendrix!
